ABOUT THE REDESIGN

Why this redesign was created

This concept was built to improve the Julius Silvert B2B ordering experience by addressing audit findings related to confusing product structure, inconsistent pricing visibility, weak inventory communication, and inefficient cart interactions.

Problem Areas Identified

The existing platform showed issues such as unclear market-price flows, missing save-for-later options, difficult quantity adjustment, product duplication confusion, and workflow friction during ordering.

Design Goals

My goal was to create a chef-centric interface that feels more premium, better organized, and more useful for restaurants and professional buyers making repeat purchases.

Key Improvements

The redesign focuses on inventory alerts, stronger category hierarchy, improved pricing clarity, and a more modern B2B visual identity.

Platform Fit

This redesign is intended for a high-volume B2B commerce platform that can support account-based pricing, repeat ordering, requisition workflows, and ERP integration.

FINAL VISION

A more modern and professional experience for serious B2B food buyers.

Instead of feeling like a crowded catalog, the redesigned Julius Silvert experience is meant to feel more like a professional ordering system that supports speed, clarity, and trust.

“The redesign is not only about making the site look better. It is about making the ordering process easier, faster, and more reliable for chefs and business buyers.”
